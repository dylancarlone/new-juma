sections:
  - heading: A nonprofit social enterprise built around young people.
    body: |-
      Juma operates real social enterprises across San Francisco, San Jose, Seattle, and Sacramento — major league sports venues and local businesses — with a singular purpose: to employ and empower young people who need it most.

      Through our YouthConnect program, Juma youth don't just get jobs. They build skills, financial knowledge, and connections that open doors for life.

      **EARN** — Real paychecks from real jobs at Juma's social enterprises across San Francisco, San Jose, Seattle, and Sacramento — major league sports venues and other local businesses — building customer service, sales, and business operations experience.

      **LEARN** — Financial literacy, resume workshops, mock interviews, and career tours to build lasting readiness for the workforce.

      **CONNECT** — Individualized coaching, the Juma Blueprint, and alumni support to navigate life transitions and reach long-term goals.
    image: 7039-photo-hero-programs-scaled.jpg
  - heading: Opportunity Youth — those who need it most.
    body: |-
      YouthConnect serves transition-aged youth (16–24) from low-income backgrounds facing systemic barriers to employment and economic mobility.

      **Foster Care Background** — Youth who have aged out of the foster care system often lack the family support networks that ease transitions into adulthood and stable work.

      **Juvenile Justice Involvement** — Young people with juvenile justice histories face unique employment barriers that Juma's model is specifically designed to help remove.

      **Low-Income & Trauma History** — 96% of Juma youth are low-income. Many face poverty and trauma cycles that — without the right intervention — can last a lifetime.

      ### 2025 Youth Demographics

      **Ethnicity:** Hispanic / Latinx 36%, Asian 24%, Black / African American 23%, Other 12%, White 5%.

      **Age:** Ages 18–20 67%, Ages 21+ 18%, Ages 16–17 15%.
    image: 7042-photo-team-scaled.jpg
  - heading: The real barriers our youth face.
    body: |-
      In 2025, Juma youth reported these challenges as active barriers in their lives — underscoring why wraparound support matters as much as job training.

      - Reduced Income / Unemployment — 122 youth
      - Mental Health — 85 youth
      - Transportation Access — 76 youth
      - Food Access — 59 youth

      **96%** of Juma youth are low-income, facing financial barriers that employment and financial capability training directly address.

      **69%** rely on government benefits for necessities like food — making Juma's wage-earning model a literal lifeline.

  - heading: Deepening our impact.
    body: |-
      **Re-launching Connect & the Juma Blueprint** — In 2025, Juma re-launched Connect — a key pillar of the YouthConnect model. This investment provides individualized coaching to help youth navigate life transitions, paired with the new Juma Blueprint: a personalized guide aligning each young person's aspirations with actionable steps toward education, employment, and entrepreneurship.

      **Listening & Evolving** — In 2025, our Learning and Evaluations team held several focus groups with youth to gain insight into their Juma experiences. That feedback is directly informing program improvements. We also expanded formal alumni services — creating new channels to hear from Juma youth long after they've graduated.
    image: ""
  - heading: From Ben & Jerry's to sports arenas.
    body: |-
      What began with a single scoop shop in San Francisco has grown into a national model for youth employment and social enterprise.

      **1993 — Founded in San Francisco:** Juma opens its first Ben & Jerry's location in San Francisco, pioneering the model of operating a real business specifically to employ and support opportunity youth.

      **Early 2000s — Expanding Into Sports Venues:** Juma grows beyond ice cream, forging partnerships with professional sports venues to operate concession businesses — creating more jobs and a richer training environment.

      **2010s — Going Multi-City:** Juma expands into Sacramento, San Jose, and Seattle, bringing the YouthConnect model to thousands more young people across the West Coast.

      **2025 — 4 Cities. 20+ Social Enterprises. Growing Impact.:** Juma now operates across 20+ social enterprise locations in San Francisco, San Jose, Sacramento, and Seattle — major league sports venues and local businesses — employing 462 youth in 2025 alone, re-launching the Connect pillar, and expanding alumni services to deepen long-term impact.
