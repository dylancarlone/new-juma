Over five million young people across the nation are not in school or working. These disconnected youth lack opportunity, not talent. At the same time, there are millions of jobs going unfilled in the workplace as employers can’t find qualified applicants. With the help of UPS and Georgia Tech, Juma is launching a youth talent pipeline pilot in Atlanta where we support young adults transition into great jobs at UPS, and connect them to Georgia Tech certification programs to help them advance to high-wage careers.

As part of this work, UPS held private interviews for Juma graduates last winter and proceeded to hire ten young adults who were ready to hit the ground running. As Juma graduates started their new UPS jobs, UPS staff saw their work in action and began to realize for themselves how their spirit and dedication contribute to team morale and productivity. “Juma youth are hardworking, collaborative, and determined, all the necessary ingredients to succeed at UPS. We are thrilled to have Juma youth join our team and help us deliver on our reputation for billions of customers everywhere.” says Ken Glaus, Human Resources Manager for UPS.

UPS and Juma embrace a shared value partnership in which young adults benefit from enriching work experiences and UPS benefits from quality talent that ultimately drives business success.

To complement the UPS job, Juma supports youth as they enroll in Georgia Tech’s Logistics Education And Pathways (LEAP) program, which serves as a fast-track certification meant to introduce youth to high-growth careers in Logistics and Supply Chain Management careers. Juma believes that by pairing work experience at UPS with an educational program, youth not only gain a college experience, they are better able to advance and grow within UPS. “When given the educational resources and support they deserve, Juma’s young adults not only meet businesses expectations, they exceed them,” says Juma’s Director of Strategic Partnerships, Sarah Thompson.

Juma looks forward to developing these partnerships and replicating this model with other partners in other cities as we continue to empower youth to create personal and social change: to build careers, advance in the labor market, enrich workplaces, and add value to businesses.
