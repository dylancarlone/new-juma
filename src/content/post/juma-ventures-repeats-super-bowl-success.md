In 2016, Super Bowl 50 came to Levi’s Stadium. It would become one of the busiest days in Juma’s history – and our youth were ready. More than 70,000 fans filled the stands hungry for hot dogs, ice cream, iced tea, and more. The young people who arrived early that morning met every customer with energy, focus, and a smile. By the end of the day, they had multiple Juma records for items sold, customers served, and revenue generated.

That cohort set the standard for composure under pressure, exceptional customer service, and the ability to rise to the moment — perfectly embodying Juma’s mission and values under some of the most demanding conditions imaginable. In the years that followed, future Jumans (many of them younger siblings, friends, and classmates of the 2016 cohort) joined the program inspired by that legacy and eager for their own chance to work a historic event.

That opportunity finally came ten years later, when the biggest event in American sports returned to Levi’s Stadium for Super Bowl 60 this past February. A new cohort of Juma youth made the early-morning commute to prepare for the exciting shift. Long before the fans, coaches, players (and even Bad Bunny!) arrived, they were already hard at work taking inventory, organizing their stations, and preparing for the long lines.

As tens of thousands of fans packed the stands, our young people rose to the ultimate test of skill, composure, and endurance.

Just like the cohort before them, Juma youth met the moment and exceeded expectations. The 60 Juma youth working that day served more than 3,000 guests, generated nearly $30,000 in sales, and earned over $10,000 in wages. These results are a powerful testament to the Juma model: young people building real, transferable skills that prepare them for even the most demanding days in the workplace.

As major events continue to come to Juma-staffed stadiums, including the World Cup later this year, our youth show up ready to earn, learn, and lead.
