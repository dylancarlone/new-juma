Meet Fiona, a UC Berkeley sophomore, multilingual linguistics researcher, and dedicated Juma Lead. Fiona joined Juma the summer after high school, just as she was navigating a complicated FAFSA application that left her with a miscalculated aid package. She needed a supportive job that wouldn’t interfere with her studies — and Juma came into her life at exactly the right time.

Since then, the stadium has become a classroom for life skills no university lecture could provide. As a Lead at the Cafe and Boba stands, Fiona has mastered the art of communication. She says that serving food at the stadiums “builds so much confidence and bravery in the sense that you can talk with anybody” — and listening to her speak at events like JumaLink, you can’t help but notice how much those skills shine.

Juma youth were put to the test on Giants Opening Day at Oracle Park. The energy was electric, the lines were endless, and Fiona and her team were adapting to several new menu items — including pastries and matcha that fans were particularly excited about. “Our line was not cut off at any point,” she says. Through the rush, the camaraderie of her team and the support of her supervisor and Juma Enterprise Manager Josh kept her grounded.

Through Juma’s Youth Ambassador program, Fiona has developed into a confident public speaker and sharpened her networking skills – delivering a speech to a room full of Juma fans last spring and putting both to work at last fall’s JumaLink networking event.

Looking ahead, Fiona is preparing to spend the summer studying abroad in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, for a six-week program immersing herself in Portuguese and Latin American literature. It’s a dream made more accessible by the financial stability and flexibility Juma provided.

At Juma, we are incredibly proud to be part of Fiona’s story as she prepares for her next big adventure in Brazil and beyond.
