Houlay (pictured second from the right) learned about Juma Seattle through a flyer posted on a bulletin board at her high school–Global Connections–and she decided to join Juma because she needed help saving money for college. She also wanted a first job that would get her out of the house and experiencing life.

Of her first experience working as a vendor at Safeco Field she says:

**“It was super duper fun and I felt like I was in my element… Working at Juma I learned responsibility, how to own up to my actions, different ways to make people smile, how to read body language, and how to achieve my goals.”**

Houlay eventually worked her way up to a Cart Manager, leading a team of other youth. In this position, she says she gained leadership skills, improved her customer service, and quality control.

Houlay has since graduated high school and moved onto a position with Amazon as an Associate. She says that her experience as a Manager with Juma helped her get the position and has helped her in her new role. “At Amazon, I have to hold others accountable and be accountable for myself as well–in a similar way to when I was at Juma.”

She is finishing up her Associate Degree in Engineering at Highline College and hopes to then transfer to a four year university. Under consideration are MIT or Washington State University.
